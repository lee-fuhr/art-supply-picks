Quick answer

For the most versatile all-in-one starter set, the Melissa & Doug Deluxe Wooden Art Set is our top pick because it combines quality materials with a thoughtful variety of mediums.

Choose the Crayola Inspiration Art Case if you want maximum pieces at a popular price; the Faber-Castell Young Artist set if you're ready to invest in premium supplies; the Art 101 Budding Artist set for a no-fuss budget option; or the Prina 50-Piece set if sketching and shading are the main focus.

A good art supply set can be the difference between frustration and creative flow for a budding artist. You want enough variety to explore drawing, coloring, and painting without overwhelming a beginner with dozens of poor-quality tools. The trick is balancing variety with quality: cheap sets often have dull pencils, chalky pastels, or crayons that break easily. A well-chosen set grows with the user, offering refillable or replaceable components. When shopping, look first at the mediums included – decent colored pencils, watercolors, pastels, and markers are a solid foundation. Next, consider the case design: a sturdy wooden or hard-plastic box that keeps everything organized is worth the extra cost. Finally, think about the age and skill level of the artist. Some sets are better for preschoolers who need oversized crayons and washable markers; others suit older kids and teens ready for sketching pencils and fine-tip pens. Our verdict

The right art set doesn’t just fill a box – it builds a creative habit. For most families, the Melissa & Doug Deluxe Wooden Art Set strikes the best balance of quality, variety, and longevity. If your budget is tighter or you need a quick starter, the Crayola Inspiration Art Case still delivers reliable fun. And for the young artist who’s ready to dive deep into drawing, the Prina set or the Faber-Castell set will support their growth without holding them back. Whichever you choose, pair it with a stack of good paper and the freedom to make messes.
